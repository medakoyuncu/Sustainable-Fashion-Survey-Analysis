## Data & Methodology
* **Sample:** 303 METU students (collected via random sampling method).
* **Data Collection:** Online and face-to-face surveys consisting of 14 questions (demographics + shopping habits).
* **Analysis Tools:** R 
### Key Statistical Methods Used:

2.  **Chi-Squared Test:** To test the independence between fast fashion consumption and sustainability awareness.

## Key Findings
* **Sustainability & Fast Fashion:** A statistically significant relationship was found between fast fashion consumption frequency and sustainability awareness ($p < 0.05$), though the relationship is weak to moderate.
* **Demographics:** No statistically significant difference was found in sustainable clothing preferences based on **gender** or **faculty**.
* **Education Impact:** The number of years spent at the university did not show a significant correlation with higher sustainability preferences.
* **Second-Hand Habits:** The majority of participants (80.53%) stated they do not buy second-hand clothes.
